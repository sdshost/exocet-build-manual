Dash Mounting

Contents / Dash Mounting
  1. Note, if you want to change gauge faces etc do this now.
  2. Cut off the white mounting tabs to allow the cluster to fit inside the gauge pod.
  3. Remove the front of the dash cluster (10 plastic clips around the edge). Store carefully as to not get damaged/dirty.
  4. When adhesive dry clip the dash back inplace. Note it will be very difficult to remove again.
  5. Using PU adhesive glue the the front of the cluster in place, ensure square and central.
  6. Add some sticky back foam to the top of the ignition barrel mount plate for the front of the dash.
  7. Sit the dash in place, add masking tape to the chassis rail and the back of the gauge pod, drop lines to enable easy lining up again.
  8. Cut a piece of 3x30mm steel to aprx 370mm.
  9. Bend the ends up aprx 30 degrees at 70mm from each end, this piece should then fit in the back of the gauge pod, tweak angles as required.
  10. Mark equal distances in from lower rear edge of pod eg 20mm in, 15mm up.
  11. Drill with a stepper drill to 6mm.
  12. Fit the steel back in place, mark the holes, indicate which side is which, remove and drill 6mm holes.
  13. Cut 2 70mm pieces of the same material.
  14. Bend this back on itself to have a internal angle of aprx 70degrees.
  15. Fit the dash in place by your lines earlier, attach the steel to the pod (behind the steering bracket)
  16. Offer the 2 small pieces between the steering bracket and the pod bracket, tweak bends as required, mark position.
  17. Remove bracket, and weld together on the marks (drill and bolt if you prefer not to weld)
  18. Refit bracket, G clamp in place, fit guage pod + adjust as required.
  19. Remove guage pod leaving bracket
  20. Drill for 2 M8 Bolts and nuts.
  21. Paint and reassemble. Remember to connect speedo cable and wiring.
